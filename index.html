A new time-domain spectral noise shaping approach for speech and audio coding

Niloofar Omidi Piralideh, Philippe Gournay, Roch Lefebvre

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Universit√© de Sherbrooke

Abstract

xxThis paper introduces a new approach to perceptually shape the spectrum of coding noise in speech and audio codecs. This approach is based on the well-known principle of pre- and post-processing using adaptive linear filters. However, it features a new way to estimate the coefficients of the post-processing filter. Unlike conventional approaches in which these coefficients are estimated from the input of the pre-processor (forward adaptation) or from the past decoded signal at the decoder side (backward adaptation), in the new approach, they are estimated from the current output of the decoder. This estimation not only requires no transmission of information but also introduces no input-filter lag. For this to work correctly, some constraints must be imposed on the pre-processor. An implementation of this new approach we refer to as constrained adaptation is described. Subjective evaluation results show that constrained adaptation performs at least as well as forward or backward adaptation in shaping coding noise but at no cost in bit rate or lag.
